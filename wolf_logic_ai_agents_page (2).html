What we build

Three layers of agent work. One coherent stack.

Most AI work falls into one of three layers. We build at all three — and we work out which one your platform needs before we propose anything.

Layer 01

Single-purpose agents

An agent that does one thing exceptionally well. Triaging support tickets. Extracting structured data from documents. Drafting compliance summaries. Watching for anomalies in your customer's data and raising flags. Each agent is scoped, evaluated, and shipped as a discrete capability your platform can call.

Examples: document intelligence · ticket triage · anomaly detection · summary generation
Layer 02

Multi-agent systems

Where one agent isn't enough. A compliance system, for instance, isn't one agent — it's a network of agents that monitor records, cross-reference regulations, draft remediation recommendations, and surface evidence for inspectors. We design these networks with the right boundaries, escalation paths, and human-in-the-loop checkpoints.

Examples: compliance frameworks · multi-stage workflows · agent orchestration · governed delegation
Layer 03

The platform underneath

Agents need a substrate. Somewhere to live, run procedures, hold state, log decisions, integrate with the rest of your business. We've built that substrate — it's called Wolfpack — and we use it to deliver our agent engagements. Your platform can run on it, integrate with it, or replace it with your own. Read on.

Examples: agentic work platform · procedures · cases · knowledge integration

Wolfpack — where agents do real work.

Wolfpack is an agentic work platform built around the idea that AI agents should be first-class citizens of an organisation, not chat windows bolted onto one. It's the substrate we use to deliver every agent engagement — and it's available as a platform you can use directly, build your own agents inside, and run with your choice of underlying model.

Think of it as the operational floor for agents: a place where they have work to do, procedures to follow, knowledge to draw on, and a record of every decision they make.

Feature 01

Build your own agents — with any model

Configure agents inside the platform. Give them named skills, procedures, and permissions. Choose the model behind each one — Claude, Gemini, DeepSeek, OpenAI, or your own — based on what the work needs. Swap models without rewriting agents.

Feature 02

Procedures, not prompts

Codify how work should be done as named procedures. Agents execute procedures, log every step, and produce audit-ready evidence. Procedures are versioned, governed, and testable — the opposite of brittle prompt chains.

Feature 03

Work items, cases, and knowledge

Agents work on the same surface as your team: a kanban of work items, a case management system for longer-running engagements, a wiki for institutional knowledge, threaded discussions for context. Humans and agents share the workplace.

Feature 04

Governed by design

Every action an agent takes is logged, attributable, and reversible. Permissions are explicit. Approval workflows are first-class. Built for the kind of buyer whose lawyers and regulators ask hard questions about what their AI is allowed to do.

What we won't build

What we're not.

Every honest page about AI should include the things its author refuses to build. So:

✕
Autonomous agents with no human in the loop For consequential decisions in regulated environments, there is always a human checkpoint. Fully autonomous decision-making in those contexts isn't responsible AI — it's a future lawsuit.
✕
Customer-facing chatbots without guardrails We won't drop a raw LLM into a customer-facing channel and call it an AI strategy. Every agent we ship has scoped capabilities, evaluation suites, and known failure modes.
✕
"AI strategy" decks for your board We don't produce slideware. If your board needs a 40-page AI strategy presentation, hire a strategy consultancy. We produce specifications, working agents, and production code.
✕
Replacement-grade AI for safety-critical decisions AI augments expert judgment in regulated work — it doesn't replace it. If your use case requires the agent to be the final word on a clinical, legal, or financial decision, we will tell you not to build it.
