What makes Wolf Logic different

Four things our competitors can't easily replicate.

When you're choosing a partner to ship AI inside a regulated platform, the stakes are higher than choosing a generalist agency. Here is what we bring that you won't find in most places — and why each of them matters specifically to the work you're trying to do.

Moat 01
Regulated AI · CQC · Multi-agent systems · CareDocs

We've shipped production AI in a regulated environment. Most haven't.

The AI agency category has exploded over the last eighteen months. Most of those agencies have built proofs of concept, internal tools, or consumer-facing chatbots. Very few have shipped production AI inside platforms that are subject to regulatory scrutiny — where a hallucination has consequences, where audit trails are non-negotiable, and where every model output has to be defensible to an inspector.

We have.

CareDocs is a UK care management SaaS platform used across residential and domiciliary care. Over twelve months, we designed and built a multi-agent AI compliance framework that runs against live care records, continuously monitoring against CQC Digital Social Care Records requirements. The system makes recommendations to care workers, flags compliance gaps to care managers, and produces audit-ready evidence — without producing the kind of confident-but-wrong outputs that disqualify most current-generation AI from regulated use.

That experience changes what we know how to do. We know how to architect AI systems with the right guardrails. We know how to write evaluation suites that prove a model is safe to ship. We know what compliance evidence regulators actually accept. None of that is on a course. It comes from having done it.

Production AI in a regulated environment is not a harder version of production AI. It's a different discipline.

Moat 02
Delivery model · Engineering-led · No handover · No bait-and-switch

The people who scope your work are the people who build it.

In larger consultancies, the engagement is sold by partners and delivered by graduates. The senior people you meet in the pitch are rarely the people you see again. This isn't a criticism — it's how the model works at scale. But it's a poor fit for technical work where the cost of misunderstanding is high.

Wolf Logic is deliberately structured differently. The engineers who lead our sprints are the engineers who lead the build. The technical conversation you have in the first meeting is with the person who will be in your codebase six months later. There is no handover from sales to delivery, because there is no separate sales function.

This has three consequences that matter. The scoping is more accurate, because the person doing it knows what they're signing up to build. The timelines are more honest, because the person quoting them is the person who will hit them. And the relationship that builds in the first conversation is the relationship that delivers the work — which matters more than people think when something inevitably needs a hard call mid-project.

Moat 03
Live migration · Service-oriented architecture · Zero-downtime · Legacy modernisation

We modernise live platforms without breaking them.

Most software firms build new systems. Greenfield projects are easier — there's no production traffic, no existing customers, no legacy data, no architectural decisions made by someone five years ago that have to be respected.

Most established vertical SaaS platforms do not have the option of greenfield. The platform works, it has customers, it generates revenue, and replacing it from scratch is a multi-year programme with unacceptable risk. What these platforms actually need is something most firms can't do: change the architecture of a running system without interrupting the business.

We can. The CareDocs platform underwent a migration from a monorepo to a service-oriented architecture during the same twelve months we were building the AI compliance framework — live, on a system serving care homes in real time, without downtime that customers noticed. This is genuinely difficult work. It requires patience, deep architectural thinking, an unusual discipline around feature flags and migration paths, and the willingness to do things slowly when speed would cause damage.

If your platform is established and your customers depend on it, "rebuild it" is rarely the right answer. We're the team that can give you a different answer.

Moat 04
AI-native team · Senior engineers · No offshore · Real velocity

Small by design. Faster because of it.

We are deliberately small. Not because we're early-stage — we've been building software for years — but because the kind of work we do is poorly served by scale. Adding people to an engineering project does not make it move faster; it usually makes it move slower. The teams that ship the cleanest work in the most complex domains are almost always small.

What makes our small team different is what we've built around it. We've invested heavily in AI-augmented engineering tooling — the same generative AI that we ship for our clients is embedded in how we work internally. A senior engineer with the right AI tooling now delivers more code, of higher quality, in less time, than the same engineer did eighteen months ago. We've spent the last year quietly figuring out how to compound this advantage. The result is a team that punches well above its headcount.

For you, this means three things. The team you meet is the team you get — no offshore overflow, no hidden subcontractors. The velocity is real — we ship at a pace most teams our nominal size cannot. And the cost structure is honest — you're not paying for a London tower, a sales floor, or a marketing budget. You're paying for engineering.

Why this combination is rare

Most firms have one of these. Almost none have all four.

Each of the four moats above can be found in other places, if you look hard enough. There are AI-native agencies. There are small teams that work directly with founders. There are infrastructure firms that handle live system migrations. There are consultancies with regulated-industry experience.

But the specific combination — production AI experience in a regulated environment, an engineering-led delivery model, the ability to modernise live platforms without breaking them, and a small, AI-augmented team that compounds — is genuinely rare in the UK market. We know, because we built it deliberately over years.

If your problem is "we need to ship AI features inside a regulated vertical SaaS platform without breaking what's already working," there are not many firms in the UK who can credibly tell you they have done this work. We are one of them.
