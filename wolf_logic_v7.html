The thesis

Most AI projects fail because the organisation isn't ready — not because the model isn't.

You don't buy AI. You absorb it. New workflows, new review disciplines, new decisions about what people still do and what they hand over. Companies that skip the absorption end up with pilots that never ship, agents nobody trusts, and a team that resents the whole thing.

We know because we went through it ourselves. The way Wolf Logic builds software today looks almost nothing like 2023. That transformation is what we now run for our customers.

How an engagement runs

From "we should do something with AI" to production.

The shape of a typical Wolf Logic engagement. Each phase has a defined deliverable — no open-ended billing, no scope drift.

Phase 01

Review

We audit your data, systems, and team. We identify the highest-impact, lowest-risk opportunities. You leave with build-ready specifications.

2 weeks · £12,500 fixed
Phase 02

Build

We design the agents, write the evaluation suites, build the guardrails, and integrate with your platform. Engineering led by the same people who scoped it.

8–16 weeks · Scoped from review
Phase 03

Deploy

We ship into production — on Wolfpack, on your existing platform, or as a hybrid. Runbooks and handover sessions so your team can own it from day one.

2–4 weeks
